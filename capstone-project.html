About the Project

This project tackles data scarcity in AI driven vehicle damage assessment by building a scalable pipeline to generate high quality synthetic car damage images. Using Stable Diffusion fine tuned with LoRA and a Mixture of Experts approach, the pipeline enables controlled generation across damage types and severity levels, producing synthetic data that overcomes real world dataset limitations and supports more reliable training of damage detection models.

Problem Statement

Real world vehicle damage datasets are small, imbalanced, and lacking in rare damage types, reducing AI model reliability. Manual data collection and labeling is costly and time consuming, particularly for uncommon damage scenarios. Together, these limitations create significant barriers to building accurate, scalable, and automated damage assessment systems for insurance claims.

Approach
  • Curated damage specific subsets from the CarDD dataset to ensure high quality and representative training samples
  • Generated structured image text pairs using a vision language model to support fine tuning and prompt development
  • Fine tuned Stable Diffusion 1.5 with LoRA adapters to train expert models tailored to individual damage types
  • Applied a Mixture of Experts approach to enable controlled and scalable generation across damage types and severity levels
  • Evaluated synthetic outputs using Frechet Inception Distance and visual inspection to measure generation quality and model improvement
Output Interpretation
  • Successfully generated realistic synthetic images across multiple vehicle damage categories with controllable severity levels
  • Demonstrated that LoRA based fine tuning enables efficient model specialization without requiring full retraining
  • Validated the feasibility of synthetic data as a supplement to real world datasets through FID based evaluation
  • Enabled broader coverage of rare damage scenarios, supporting more robust and reliable training of downstream damage detection models
